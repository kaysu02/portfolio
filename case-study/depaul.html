DePaul Digital Transformation

Reimagining the digital student experience to improve usability, accessibility, and engagement across key university touchpoints.

Role Lead Product Designer
Team 4 Designers, 2 PMs
Duration 6 months
Tools Figma, Maze, Miro

The Challenge

DePaul University's digital ecosystem had grown organically over years, resulting in a fragmented student experience. Students reported high frustration and frequently resorted to in-person visits for tasks that should have been self-service.

  • Key workflows spread across disconnected systems with inconsistent UI patterns
  • Poor mobile support despite 68% of students accessing services on mobile
  • Accessibility gaps that failed to meet WCAG 2.1 AA standards
  • 62% task failure rate on the course registration flow
Challenge overview image

The Design Process

I led research and iterative design across the full project lifecycle — from discovery through validation — combining qualitative insights with rapid prototyping to build solutions grounded in real student needs.

  • Conducted 18 in-depth student interviews and analyzed 2 years of help desk data
  • Created journey maps for 4 key personas, identifying critical drop-off points
  • Built a unified design system with reusable components for consistency
  • Redesigned information architecture using card sorting with 45 participants
  • Ran 3 rounds of usability testing (n=8 per round), iterating between each cycle
  • Applied progressive disclosure, a unified dashboard, and contextual inline help
Design process image

Impacts

The redesigned digital experience delivered measurable improvements across usability, support load, and student satisfaction — validating the research-driven approach and building momentum for continued transformation.

  • 40% increase in task completion across key student workflows
  • 55% reduction in help desk tickets related to digital services
  • Student satisfaction score improved to 4.2/5
  • Stakeholder alignment workshops established a model for future cross-team collaboration
