Texas Childcare Portal CMS Design

Designing a content management system for the Texas Childcare Portal to streamline how administrators publish, manage, and organize child care resources statewide.

Role Product Designer
Sector Government
Platform Web

The Challenge

Administrators managing Texas child care resources relied on outdated, manual processes to publish and organize content — creating bottlenecks that delayed critical information from reaching families and providers.

  • No proper content management system — updates required technical support
  • Slow, error-prone publishing workflow from drafting to review to publication
  • No structured taxonomy for organizing resources across the portal
  • Administrators couldn't preview content before it went live
Challenge overview image

The Design Process

I conducted stakeholder interviews and workflow analysis to understand how content moved through the organization, revealing key inefficiencies and opportunities to streamline the editorial process.

  • Mapped the end-to-end content lifecycle from drafting through publication
  • Designed an intuitive WYSIWYG content editor for non-technical administrators
  • Built role-based permissions to support multi-team editorial workflows
  • Created a structured taxonomy for organizing child care resources statewide
  • Implemented a live preview system so administrators see content as it will appear
Design process image

Impacts

The new CMS empowered administrators to publish and manage content independently, significantly reducing turnaround times and eliminating the technical bottleneck.

  • Administrators can publish content without technical support
  • Content turnaround time significantly reduced
  • Improved content accuracy through structured workflows and preview
  • Easier to keep statewide child care resources up to date and organized
