Government Web Design Accessibility

Texas Workforce Commission Child Care Websites

Redesigning public-facing child care websites to help Texas families find, understand, and access child care resources more easily.

Role Product Designer
Sector Government
Platform Web

The Challenge

Texas families seeking child care resources faced a confusing web of outdated government websites, making it difficult for parents who needed help the most to find and access available support.

  • Critical information about subsidies and eligibility buried across multiple disconnected sites
  • Inconsistent navigation patterns and dense legal language
  • Poor mobile experiences despite most users accessing on phones
  • High drop-off rates on key resource pages
Challenge overview image

The Design Process

I partnered with stakeholders across TWC to understand the landscape of child care services and the diverse needs of Texas families, then led the redesign from research through implementation.

  • Conducted user research with parents, providers, and caseworkers to map pain points
  • Performed content audits to identify redundant, outdated, and confusing information
  • Redesigned information architecture with clear hierarchy and plain-language content
  • Built responsive layouts optimized for the devices families actually use
  • Validated designs through usability testing with target audiences
Design process image

Impacts

The redesigned websites made it significantly easier for Texas families to find child care resources and take action — reducing confusion and increasing engagement with key services.

  • Streamlined navigation reduced time-to-task for finding child care resources
  • Plain-language content improved comprehension of eligibility requirements
  • Responsive design increased mobile engagement
  • Reduced support inquiries related to website confusion
